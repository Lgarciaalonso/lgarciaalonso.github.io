About the Researcher

PhD Candidate @ UPV/EHU

I am a second-year PhD Candidate in Comparative Literature at the University of the Basque Country (UPV/EHU), specializing in the intersection of Contemporary Gothic Studies and Mythocriticism. My doctoral research, titled The Shadow of Adulthood, examines the structural use of Gothic conventions and the classical myth of Descensus ad Inferos (Katabasis) within J.K. Rowling’s Harry Potter saga. My work seeks to establish a definitive correlation between the evolution of narrative darkness and the protagonist’s psychological maturation.

Beyond my primary research, I am deeply interested in the Digital Humanities and the impact of emerging technologies on literary analysis and pedagogical practices. This dual focus informs my professional background as an ESOL educator, where I have spent several years bridging the gap between linguistic instruction and literary engagement. I view the study of English Language and Literature not merely as an academic pursuit, but as a dynamic field where tradition and digital innovation converge.

Currently, I am focused on developing the theoretical framework for my dissertation while actively participating in international conferences to discuss the enduring relevance of the Gothic in 21st-century fiction.

Supervised by "Dr. Alejandro Martínez Sobrino" (UPV/EHU) & Dra. Miriam Borham Puyal (USAL).
